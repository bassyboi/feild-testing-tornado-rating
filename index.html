Tornado Danger Rating Calculator

About This Tool

This calculator estimates a tornado danger rating (0–10) based on three key field observations:

  • CAPE (J/kg): The energy available for storm development. More energy means more potential for strong updrafts.
  • Bulk Wind Shear (s⁻¹): How quickly winds speed up with height. Greater shear helps storms develop rotation.
  • Storm Tilt Angle (°): The observed tilt of the storm. A stronger tilt often indicates a developing mesocyclone (rotating updraft).

Supercells are organized storms with rotating updrafts (mesocyclones) and are more likely to produce tornadoes. In contrast, "popcorn" (or pulse) storms are scattered and short-lived with little rotation.

Enter the values you observe in the field and click the "Calculate Tornado Danger Rating" button. The resulting score will give you a quick indication of tornado potential:

  • 0–3: Low tornado risk
  • 4–6: Moderate tornado risk
  • 7–10: High tornado risk

How to Measure in the Field

CAPE: Often estimated using weather balloon (radiosonde) data or local observations.

Shear: Measured between low and mid-levels (e.g., near the surface and at 6000 m) using mobile weather equipment.

Tilt Angle: Can be visually estimated or determined by radar analysis of the storm’s structure.
